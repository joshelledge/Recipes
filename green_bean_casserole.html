Green Bean Casserole with Fresh Ingredients

Description: A classic green bean casserole made with fresh ingredients, creamy sauce, and topped with crispy onions. Perfect for Thanksgiving!

Ingredients

  • 40 ounces fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 8 ounces fresh mushrooms, chopped
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk (whole or 2%)
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup crispy fried onions (store-bought or homemade)
  • Optional: 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese, grated

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F. Prepare a 9x13-inch baking dish by lightly greasing it.
  2. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Blanch the green beans for 2-3 minutes until bright green. Drain and transfer to an ice bath to stop cooking.
  3. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add mushrooms and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Stir in flour and cook for 1-2 minutes until golden. Gradually whisk in milk and chicken broth, cooking until thickened.
  5. Season the sauce with nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Stir in the cooked mushrooms.
  6. Combine the blanched green beans and sauce in the baking dish, spreading evenly. Sprinkle crispy onions and Parmesan cheese (if using) on top.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until bubbling and golden on top. Serve warm.
