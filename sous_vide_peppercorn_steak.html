Sous Vide Peppercorn Steaks

Description: Perfectly cooked sous vide peppercorn steaks with a tender medium-rare center, finished with a crisp peppercorn crust, garlic butter, and fresh herbs.

Ingredients

  • 3 small steaks (e.g., filet mignon, ribeye, or sirloin)
  • 2 tablespoons black peppercorns, crushed
  • 1 tablespoon kosher salt
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 3 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 1 tablespoon high-smoke point oil (e.g., avocado oil)
  • Flaky salt, for finishing

Instructions

  1. Set up the sous vide machine to 130°F (54°C) for medium-rare steaks. If you prefer medium, set it to 135°F (57°C).
  2. Pat the steaks completely dry with paper towels. Season generously on both sides with kosher salt and crushed peppercorns.
  3. Place each steak in a vacuum-sealed or resealable freezer bag, removing as much air as possible.
  4. Submerge the sealed steaks into the sous vide bath and cook for 90 minutes. (The sous vide ensures an even cook without overcooking, even if slightly left longer.)
  5. After 90 minutes, remove the steaks from the bags and pat dry thoroughly to ensure a perfect sear.
  6. Heat a cast-iron skillet or heavy-bottomed pan over high heat until very hot. Add high-smoke point oil.
  7. Place the steaks into the pan. Sear for 1 minute per side to develop a golden crust.
  8. Add butter, smashed garlic, and fresh herbs (rosemary and thyme) to the skillet. Tilt the pan slightly and spoon the melted butter over the steaks for 30 seconds to infuse flavor.
  9. Remove the steaks and let them rest for 5 minutes before serving. Finish with flaky salt for added texture and flavor.

Notes

The sous vide method ensures the steaks are perfectly cooked to your desired temperature with no guesswork. Make sure to pat the steaks completely dry after cooking to achieve the best crust during searing. Resting the steaks is essential to lock in their juices.
