Thanksgiving Turkey with Brine and Herbs

Description: A perfectly brined and herb-seasoned Thanksgiving turkey, crisped for golden skin and juicy, flavorful meat.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole turkey (27 lbs)
  • 1 brine kit (or homemade brine solution)
  • 1/4 cup kosher salt (for the brine)
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1 gallon water
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 2 sprigs sage
  • 1 sprig parsley
  • 4 tablespoons butter, melted
  • Optional: 1 onion, quartered
  • Optional: 2 cups vegetable broth

Instructions

  1. Prepare the brine: Mix water, kosher salt, and brown sugar in a large pot. Heat until dissolved and let cool completely.
  2. Submerge the turkey in the brine solution, ensuring it is fully covered. Refrigerate for 12-24 hours.
  3. Remove turkey from the brine and pat completely dry with paper towels. Allow it to air dry in the refrigerator uncovered for 6-12 hours to ensure crispy skin.
  4. Preheat the oven to 325°F. If using an oven bag, prepare it per package instructions.
  5. Rub the turkey with olive oil and melted butter. Season generously with fresh rosemary, thyme, sage, and parsley.
  6. Place the turkey breast-side down in the roasting pan or oven bag. Add optional onion and vegetable broth to the pan.
  7. Roast at 325°F for approximately 15 minutes per pound (about 4.5 hours for a 27 lb turkey). Flip the turkey breast-side up during the final hour of cooking.
  8. Optional: Increase oven temperature to 400°F for the last 10-15 minutes to crisp the skin.
  9. Remove turkey from the oven and rest for at least 30 minutes before carving.
