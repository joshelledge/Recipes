STK-Inspired Corn Pudding

Author: Josh Elledge

Description: A creamy and savory corn pudding inspired by the STK restaurant.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cornmeal (fine or medium grind)
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 1/2 cups corn kernels (fresh or thawed frozen)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh chives, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a cast iron skillet or baking dish with butter.
  2. In a saucepan, combine cornmeal, milk, and heavy cream. Whisk constantly over medium heat until thickened (5-7 minutes).
  3. Remove from heat. Stir in melted butter, sugar, salt, and pepper. Let cool slightly, then whisk in eggs.
  4. Fold in corn kernels and Parmesan cheese until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into the prepared skillet. Smooth the top and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the edges are golden and the center is set.
  6. Garnish with fresh chives and serve warm.

Nutrition

Calories: 320 | Fat: 18g | Carbs: 32g | Protein: 7g

Notes

- Whisking: Constant whisking is critical to avoid lumps in the cornmeal.
- Skillet vs Dish: A cast iron skillet gives the pudding golden edges but a baking dish works fine.
- Serving Suggestions: Best served warm alongside a hearty steak or roasted chicken.
- **Texture Note:** Inspired by our discussion, this pudding relies on fine or medium cornmeal rather than blending the corn to preserve texture.
- **Learning:** This was developed as part of our "Steak School" — where we experiment and perfect side dishes for steak dinners.

Serving Size: 6 | Total Time: 50 minutes
